PERMIT TO WORK PROCEDURE - COKE OVEN BATTERY MAINTENANCE
Document Ref: SOP-COB-014
Effective Date: 14-Jan-2025

1. SCOPE
This procedure applies to all maintenance, inspection, and confined space entry
activities carried out on Coke Oven Battery No. 3 (Equipment Tag: COB-03-MAIN).

2. HAZARD IDENTIFICATION
Coke oven batteries generate hazardous gases including carbon monoxide and
hydrogen sulfide. Gas detector GD-COB03-07 must show readings below 35 ppm CO
before any hot work permit is issued in the vicinity.

3. PERMIT REQUIREMENTS
Before issuing a hot work permit within 50 meters of COB-03-MAIN, the shift
supervisor must verify:
- Current gas detector readings from GD-COB03-07
- No confined space entry permit is active within the same zone
- PPE: FR coveralls, SCBA standby, gas monitor on person

4. EMERGENCY RESPONSE
In the event of a gas alarm (>50ppm CO), all personnel must evacuate to
Assembly Point AP-4 immediately. Shift supervisor notifies Control Room within
2 minutes. Reference: Factory Act 1948, Section 38 (Precautions in case of fire).

5. REGULATORY REFERENCE
This procedure complies with OISD-STD-105 (Process Safety Management) and
DGMS Circular 7/2019 on confined space entry in metallurgical plants.

6. INSPECTION SCHEDULE
Gas detectors GD-COB03-01 through GD-COB03-12 are inspected weekly by the
Instrumentation team. Calibration records maintained per ISO 9001 QMS.
